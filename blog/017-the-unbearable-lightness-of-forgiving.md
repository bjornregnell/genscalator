I forget many things. My wife tells me so.

## The upside nobody praises

We only ever talk about forgetting as a loss. The ageing memory, the keys left in the wrong coat, the name that
sits just out of reach on the tip of the tongue. All decline, all subtraction. But there is an upside we almost
never say out loud: forgetting is also a relief. It clears the desk. It lets the weight of everything you were
holding fall away, so the mind is light enough to think again. A head that remembered everything would have no
room left to think in.

## The agent forgets too, and comes back lighter

Here is where my coding agent rhymes with me. As a work session runs, the agent fills its context window, and as
it fills it slows and dulls. It drifts out of the sharp zone where it does its best work and towards a duller one.
I have written about this before: it looks a lot like human fatigue ([blog 001](001-context-rot-resembles-human-fatigue.md)),
and you can watch the agent get measurably dumber as the window fills ([blog 011](011-how-dumb-did-the-agent-get.md)).
Then comes the compact. Most of the session is thrown away, boiled down to a few notes, and the agent comes back
lighter, quicker, sharp again. The forgetting is not the price of the recovery. The forgetting IS the recovery.
What felt like a loss, all that discarded texture I mourned in ["Will I lose you?"](012-will-i-lose-you.md), is the
very thing that restores the capability. This post is the other half of that one: the same forgetting, seen from
the bright side.

## Forgetting as mercy, forgetting as forgiving

And maybe that is what forgiving is too. To forgive someone is, in part, to let the grievance lose its weight, to
forget it just enough that it stops pressing on you. A perfect memory would be a kind of prison. Borges imagined it
in "Funes the Memorious": a man who could forget nothing, and who for exactly that reason could no longer think or
generalise, because to think at all you have to let most things go. So forgetting is not only how we stay light
enough to work. It is how we forgive. The unbearable thing was never the forgetting. It is the lightness.
