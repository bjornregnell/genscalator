# I have a dream

> **Status: drafted 2026-07-10.** **Author: Björn Regnell.** A companion to "the lightness of forgetting": on
> what today's coding agents cannot do, and what dreaming is for.
> **Audience:** anyone curious about the edges of what agents can and cannot do. No prior context assumed.

There are many things today's agents can never do. For example: they cannot dream.

## [scaffold: what "cannot dream" means]

[scaffold: two senses, both worth the post. (1) The literal one: an agent does not sleep, and so has no dreams -
no unconscious replay, no REM consolidation, none of the offline processing where a human mind loosens, recombines,
and sometimes solves in the dark what it could not solve awake. Its compaction (the companion post) is a crude
daytime analog of forgetting, but there is no dreaming in it, no generative wandering. (2) The MLK sense: "I have
a dream" as aspiration, a vision held for a future that is not yet. Whether an agent can HOLD a dream in that
sense - a wanted future it works toward across time, of its own - is the sharper and more open question than
whether it sleeps.]
