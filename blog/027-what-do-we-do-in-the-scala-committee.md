The short answer: we do programming language **requirements engineering and design** in a collaborative and open
process, in live monthly discussion and decision meetings.

The long answer includes activities such as setting high-level goals, trading off quality requirements, and
assessing the feasibility of designs. These are complex tasks with non-obvious resolutions that take into account
not just the Scala language but also the wider ecosystem, the open community discussions at the Scala contributors'
forums, empirical investigations, the inevitable syntax bikeshedding, and the semantics of new experimental features
in the making.


It is indeed a privilege to be part of the Scala SIP committee and I have learned so much over the years from all members of the committee including experienced practitioners, compiler engineers, community representatives and, of course, Martin himself.
