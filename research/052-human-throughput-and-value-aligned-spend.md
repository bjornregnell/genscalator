# RT052 — The human-throughput bottleneck and value-aligned spend in fast-agent collaboration (the super-harness's purpose)

**Status:** open research topic, seeded 2026-07-08 from BR's brain-dump (`wr-data/br-throughput-and-token-stress-2026-07-08.md`).
This is the **motivation topic** that SM016 (super-harness) + SM022 (dashboard) are the intervention for. Parent/sibling
of RT049 (delegation dilemma), [[agent-affective-analogs]], and the PRD goal `avoidReviewOverload`.

## The problem (as stated by the human)
When the agent (CO4) is much faster than the human, the human's two scarce capacities become the rate limiter:
**(1) direction generation** — finding work that genuinely advances the over-arching direction without derailing
into low-value depth; and **(2) review** — adjudicating agent output before it can become value. Excess agent
capacity then converts into **loose ends**, not value, and the human feels **stressed** and becomes the
**bottleneck**. Anthropic's multi-level token-reset economics (session / weekly / all-models / Fable5,
use-it-or-lose-it) add an exogenous **spend-pressure + FOMO** that competes with *spending well*.

## The objective function (the values anchor)
BR's explicit target: **maximize external value for the greater good per unit of joint human+agent effort** — NOT
maximize tokens spent (the FOMO trap), NOT minimize cost. This objective *dissolves* part of the FOMO: unspent
tokens are not lost value if the alternative was low-value depth.

## Research questions
- **RQ1 (direction throughput):** how to keep the human supplied with vetted high-value work so agent capacity is
  not idle nor mis-spent? (Instruments: the PB / AFK menu as a direction-queue the *agent* stocks;
  [[keep-afk-menu-stocked]].)
- **RQ2 (review throughput):** how to keep review off the human's critical path? (Instruments: sub-agents review
  sub-agents — the delegation dance extended; batching output into fewer, larger review units; pre-reviewed
  delivery. RT049's review-cost ledger.)
- **RQ3 (spend-value alignment):** what signal tells the pair they are spending tokens on high external value?
  (A **value-per-spend** metric, not raw burn; a **loose-ends / review-queue depth** meter = the human's actual
  load. These become the dashboard's primary KPIs, SM022.)
- **RQ4 (affect):** the billing model as a stressor on the human half — can making value-delivered visible (vs
  tokens-remaining) reduce FOMO-stress and raise confidence? (The dashboard as an affective/confidence instrument,
  not just a metrics board. Links [[agent-affective-analogs]] — now with a human-affect instance.)

## Why measurable / groundable
The bottleneck is observable (agent commit-rate vs human review-rate vs loose-end accrual over a session — this
session is a specimen). Value-per-spend needs a value proxy (hard, but approachable: shipped-and-ratified artifacts
per token, or BR relevance member-check). Affect is self-reported by BR (ground truth for his own state) and can be
correlated with queue-depth. So RT052 is not just philosophy: its RQs have candidate DVs.

## Meta (047)
BR's stress is longitudinal case-study data: the human half of the joint system under an affective load from the
collaboration economics — the mirror of the agent-rot findings. The study's subject (managing a fast intelligence)
is playing out in its own conduct.
