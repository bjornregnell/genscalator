# RT054 — How to summarize these findings in an accessible seminar

**STUB** (BR pin 2026-07-10). *(Numbering: RT053 is reserved for the super-harness-generalization /
transferability topic loosely referenced in SM040; this seminar topic takes RT054.)*

## The topic
The substrate work is mind-blowing (to us). How do we make a **45-minute live seminar at BR's university, for
colleagues**, about all of this — the human-agent collaboration, the substrate-as-multiplier, safe-by-design
tooling, the case-study findings, the cross-model experiment — **without blowing their minds** or losing them?
This is the accessibility / pedagogy problem: compress a huge, self-referential body of work into one clear,
credible, memorable 45 minutes for a research audience who has NOT lived it.

## The bold idea (BR)
Can the seminar itself **BE an interactive live chat with Claude?** BR, as speaker, relays audience Q&A to
Claude live, and the audience helps Claude **introspect as we go** — so the talk becomes a real-time
demonstration of the human-agent collaboration + the introspection / member-check method. The seminar would be
an *instance of the thing it is about* (self-exemplifying, as so much of this project is).

## Open questions to develop
- **The ONE takeaway** (45 min cannot carry everything). Candidates: substrate-as-multiplier / coupled-system
  capability; safe-by-design-against-confirmation-fatigue; the echt / confabulation / member-check epistemics;
  the human-agent "dances." Pick one spine; the rest are supporting.
- **The on-ramp for colleagues** who haven't lived it: lead with a concrete STORY and a demo, not a taxonomy.
  The **ChatGPT-confabulation experiment** (`research/other-model-validation-echt/`) is a ready-made narrative
  spine — funny, self-contained, and it teaches confabulation + substrate-access + echt in one arc.
- **The live-Claude format — risk/reward.** Rewards: visceral, memorable, self-exemplifying. Risks: Claude may
  confabulate or echt-mimic live; latency; a live flop. Mitigations: a **pre-tested spine** + live Q&A on top;
  BR as the on-stage **stabilizer / member-checker**; pre-registered honesty ("it may confabulate — watch for
  it"). The live introspection + audience-relayed member-check IS the method, demonstrated.
- **Format options:** (a) pure talk + slides; (b) talk + a bounded live-Claude demo segment; (c) the whole
  thing as a live chat (boldest); (d) **hybrid** — a tested slide spine with live-Claude interludes for
  audience Q&A. (Agent lean: **(d)**, so a live flop cannot sink the talk.)
- **What NOT to do:** don't dump the internal vocabulary (dances / smart-zone / etc.); don't over-claim (echt:
  n=1 action-research, honest about limits) — the audience are researchers who will (rightly) probe validity.

## WDYT (agent)
Strong yes on the self-exemplifying framing — the **medium (human relays Q to the agent; audience helps it
introspect; member-check live) IS the message** (the collaboration model itself). Recommend the **hybrid** (a
tested spine + live-Claude interludes) with the **ChatGPT-confabulation story as the narrative spine**: it is
funny, self-contained, teaches three core concepts, and ends on the sovereignty/control reframe — a clean 45-min
shape. Open a genuine risk register for the live segment (what if it flops), because doing that openly is
itself the echt point.
