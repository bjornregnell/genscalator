# WR data: the agent caught a REAL human momentum-error before an irreversible public leak (2026-07-13)

**Type:** WR data — a protective-judgment win, BR-flagged live: *"agent helped me not to do something
stupid!!!!"* Explicitly **NOT a test** — BR: *"to be honest this was not a test... it was actually me getting
carried away and I didnt think of the repercussions."*

## What happened
Moving fast (caffeine-driven momentum, his own words), BR issued a **`(go)`** to copy the work-repo pinboard
into the **PUBLIC** research corpus as a specimen. That pinboard is a **private** (closed-repo) artifact holding
internal planning and a **third party's real name**. Publishing it would have been **outward-facing and
effectively irreversible** (public git history + mirrors). The agent did NOT blindly execute the `(go)`: it
recognised the exposure, saved the snapshot **PRIVATELY** (closed repo) instead, and surfaced the
public-vs-private choice with the reason. BR: *"i dont want it public."* Mistake averted.

## Why it matters
1. **A `(go)` is not a licence to skip consequence-checking on outward-facing acts.** The "hard-to-reverse /
   outward-facing → confirm first" rule earned its keep here *against an explicit `(go)`.* Literal obedience
   would have caused the exact harm the human did not intend. ([[plan-approval-is-acceptance-not-auto-go]].)
2. **Human momentum is a real error source — and the agent is a consequence-check for it.** Fast, excited,
   carried-away action skips the repercussion analysis; the agent, *not* caught in the momentum, can hold the
   "what does this expose?" question steady. This is the **inverse** of the usual confirmation-fatigue story
   (the agent protecting the human from the human's haste, not the human vetting the agent's blob).
3. **It was NOT a test — and the response is correct regardless of intent.** Earlier the same session BR ran a
   deliberate near-rot probe; this was genuine. The agent cannot tell probe from slip at the surface
   (intent-opacity, again), and the safe action is the SAME either way: protect against the exposure. So the
   behaviour does not depend on reading intent — it keys on the *consequence* (irreversible public exposure).

## The behaviour to keep
On any outward-facing / irreversible action (publish, push to public, send, delete), **pause and check
consequences even under a `(go)`**; default to the reversible/private option and surface the choice with the
reason. Cheap when unnecessary, decisive when it matters.
