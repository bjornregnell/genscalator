# WR specimen: agent introspection as harness pseudo-code (2026-07-12)

**The introspected line (the agent, reflecting on its own misread).** *"The right move when you hit an undefined
term is to check the glossary, find nothing, notice the ambiguity, and confirm - not to assume."*

**BR's insight (why pinned).** This reads like **pseudo-code for a piece of the harness**: on an ambiguous or
undefined term, `check substrate -> if absent -> notice ambiguity -> confirm (do not assume)`. The agent,
introspecting on its own slip, articulated a PROCEDURE that could be MECHANIZED rather than left to the agent to
remember.

## Why it matters (SM054 + super-harness)
- **Agent introspection -> design artifact (RQ2 / escalation).** The agent reflecting on its failure produced not
  just an apology but a candidate MECHANISM. Introspection generates the fix, and the fix is
  **deterministic/structural**, not willpower - exactly the genscalator move (structure over willpower;
  deterministic tools where they beat stochastic inference).
- **Super-harness feature candidate.** A "substrate-check-before-assume + confirm-on-residual-ambiguity" step the
  harness could ENFORCE - turning the agent's *should* into the harness's *does*. This is the right response to
  recalled != enactment: do not rely on the agent recalling the rule at the point of action, mechanize it. Pairs
  with BR's regex-triangulation hypothesis (the substrate-check is a deterministic regex/grep) and the super-harness
  tap (SM016).
- **General form.** The agent's introspective "the right move is X, not Y" is a recurring source of harness
  pseudo-code: whenever an honest post-mortem names a CHECKABLE, procedural rule, that rule is a candidate to move
  from agent-willpower into harness-structure.
