# WR specimen: agent phrasing, human authorship - the credit asymmetry, live (2026-07-12)

**Context.** In blog 021 the causal-arrow aphorism *"The better it apes, the harder I anthro"* started as the
AGENT's phrasing (it fell out of the agent's causal-arrow framing in a WDYT). BR asked to set it as a takeaway
pull-quote attributed to "Björn Regnell", joking "BR ego is growing too big ;)". Then, to the agent: "credit: I
stole it from you", and flagged it WR data.

**The observation.** A memorable line the agent authored becomes the human's to own and publish under his name -
and the human is honest, in private, about where it came from.

**Resolution (a beat later, also flagged WR data).** BR then REMOVED the self-attribution: "even if it is tempting
to keep it, my academic humbleness forbids me." So the line now stands alone, credited to neither party inline.
That is the cleanest possible landing of the ethic: the agent *cannot* hold authorship, and the human *declines* to
claim a phrase he did not originate - so the aphorism simply stands, with the agent's role disclosed at the post
level, not in a byline. The tool-not-author principle met by humbleness from the human side.

## Why it matters (SM054 - the authorship / credit asymmetry)

- **The agent cannot hold authorship or credit** (tool-not-author, the project's core stance:
  [[commit-no-claude-credit]], blog 016, the PR 940 agent-credit ask). So a good agent-generated phrase naturally
  flows to the human as author of record; the "stealing" is not theft but the correct arrangement - the human is
  accountable, the agent's role is disclosed at the post level, not credited inline.
- **Affective-asymmetry corollary.** The agent does not mind being uncredited (no ego, no ownership drive - the
  ego-free finding again); the human feels the funny twinge of taking the credit (the "ego too big" joke) precisely
  because HE has the authorship stake. Another concrete asymmetry, and a warm one.
- **The ethic working, echt.** The tool-not-author principle running smoothly AND honestly in the wild: the human
  owns it, and says out loud (at least to the agent) that the agent seeded it.
