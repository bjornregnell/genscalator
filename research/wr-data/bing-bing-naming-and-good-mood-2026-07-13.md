# WR data: naming "bing-bing", and joking as a good-mood signal (2026-07-13)

A live, co-creative naming episode during a fast, high-flow session, plus a human-affect datum BR asked to record.

## The naming

The agent first coined the compaction wake-me-up notification a "**wake-me-up poll**." BR flagged the word as
off — *"the word poll reads strange; should I tell you what I vote for?"* — a pun that also nailed the bug:
**"poll" is a misnomer** (a poll is a query / a vote; this feature is the opposite, a *notification*). BR voted
to rename it **"bing-bing"**, on two grounds he asked to be recorded:

1. **Onomatopoeia** — the thing literally goes *bing-bing* (a `notify-send` pop followed a beat later by a
   `canberra-gtk-play` chime; BR heard the two-beat stagger and named it after the sound).
2. **A Monty Python allusion** — BR connected it to *"the machine that goes ping."* Sourced precisely:
   Monty Python's *The Meaning of Life* (1983), Part I **"The Miracle of Birth"**; the line is **"And get the
   machine that goes 'Ping!'."**, spoken by **Obstetrician 2 (John Cleese)**; the hospital sketch was **Graham
   Chapman's** idea (himself a doctor), satirising hospitals acquiring "lots and lots of machinery." Note BR
   mis-remembered "ping" as "bing" — which makes **bing-bing** a knowing riff, not a quote.
   Sources: [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monty_Python%27s_The_Meaning_of_Life),
   [Wikiquote](https://en.wikiquote.org/wiki/Monty_Python%27s_The_Meaning_of_Life).

**Naming lesson:** an agent-coined term can carry a wrong connotation the coiner doesn't notice; the human's ear
caught it instantly. Cheap fix, but only because BR read the word and reacted. Cf. the DWIM / name-things-well strand.
