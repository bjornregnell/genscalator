## The asymmetry (three parts)
1. **Agents never get bored.** No felt under-stimulation — another entry in the affective-analog asymmetry catalog
   alongside fatigue, laughter, offense (each a human state with NO functional agent analog). "You got bored
   waiting?" is anthropomorphization: the agent has no felt wait (no felt time between turns), so there is no boredom
   to have.
2. **Bored ≈ tired-adjacent.** A bored human resembles a tired human — both are depleted / under-stimulated states —
   so boredom plausibly belongs to the same HUMAN-fatigue family the mode grammar's `tired?` infers. But the PROXY
   likely differs: fatigue shows as typos / slowing; boredom may show as restlessness, topical drift, or rapid
   idea-spawning. Candidate: a `bored?` sibling to `tired?`, or fold boredom into the tired-adjacent cluster.
3. **The generative twist — boredom is the mother of invention.** Unlike fatigue, which only DEGRADES, boredom has a
   GENERATIVE upside: human under-stimulation drives wandering, association, and invention (cf.
   [[human-associative-ideation-from-typing-friction-2026-07-12]]). So this asymmetry has an EDGE the others lack —
   the missing agent-analog is not purely a deficit to compensate; the human's boredom is a CREATIVE ENGINE the
   tireless agent structurally cannot supply.

## Why it matters (the coupling read)
This sharpens the coupled-capability finding ([[jointly-discovered-mode-grammar-coupled-capability-2026-07-15]]): the
productive division of labour maps onto the asymmetry — the HUMAN brings **boredom-driven invention** (restless
ideation, the generative spark), the AGENT brings **tireless grounding** (never bored, never fatigued, always ready
to verify + capture). The agent's lack of boredom is at once a FEATURE (tireless, no drift from under-stimulation)
and a LIMIT (no boredom-spark of its own); the pairing covers both. This very session's rapid human ideation may
itself be partly boredom / restlessness-driven invention, grounded by a tireless agent.
