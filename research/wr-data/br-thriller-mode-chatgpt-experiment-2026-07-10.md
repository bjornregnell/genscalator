# BR self-reported deep in "thriller mode" during the ChatGPT cross-model experiment (2026-07-10)

BR member-check (he is ground truth for his own state): "BR was deep into thriller mode." A brief account of
what drove it and the agent's stabilizer response.

## What happened (brief)
An unplanned live experiment caught fire. BR put genscalator's two questions (what is truly novel; why has
Anthropic not done similar) to BOTH ChatGPT and CO4 for an echt cross-model contrast (SM040). It became a fast,
thrilling back-and-forth over many short interleaved messages: CO4's grounded answers, then ChatGPT's confident
**confabulation** (a fluent made-up description of a repo it had not read), then its rounds of retraction as it
admitted it could not fetch the repo (Codeberg, then even the GitHub mirror), the sovereignty-vs-reach irony it
"appreciated" without reading anything, BR's delight ("Human laughing out loud"), the "parrot" realization,
building an open ZIP plus a redacted complement, and a Fable-5 safety audit before upload. Pace + novelty +
humour stacked into a high-arousal, absorbed state. BR named it himself: "this is too exciting, lets take a
deep breath."

## The tell: a misread under speed
A concrete thriller-mode symptom surfaced. BR read a paste-to-ChatGPT prompt (fenced in `---`) as a message
addressed to HIM, then caught it ("AHA! I misunderstood, I did not see the ---"). High arousal plus a racing
feed degraded careful reading of boundaries and audience - a small but real cognitive-narrowing effect. It
directly motivated the copy-paste-frame-rule upgrade (prominent, destination-labelled fences at BOTH ends).

## Agent role: stabilizer (the mutual-help thesis, live)
When BR called for a breath, CO4 downshifted deliberately - matched the energy DOWN, reassured that everything
was committed and nothing was time-pressured, and named it a good stopping point. Blog 001's mutual-help thesis
enacted: the human steadies the rotting agent; the agent steadies the excited or tiring human. Here the agent
was the stabilizer for a human in thriller mode.

## Echt note on the asymmetry
Unlike the agent (whose self-report is confabulation-prone - see *Corroboration asymmetry*), BR IS the
authority on his own affective state, so his self-report "I was in thriller mode" is a valid member-check
datum, not a confabulation. That is the human side of the introspection asymmetry: the human can report his own
state; the agent cannot reliably report its own - so the pair covers both blind spots. Ties: blog 011 (the
thriller-to-manic thesis), RT011 (human-state-and-joint-zone), [[agent-affective-analogs]], the mutual-help
thesis (blog 001), and the racing-feed comms hazard that triggered the copy-paste-frame-rule upgrade.
