# WR data — the collaboration is improving BR's English; and: match BR's register in publications, 2026-07-07

**Event.** Reviewing the blog, BR noted he "would never have used 'arm' in that way" (the research sense of
*study arm* / *trial arm* = a branch/condition of a study) and that he "today learned" the term from the
agent. He flagged, honestly, his own "bad (but, thanks to the agent, improving) English" and asked the agent
to consider simplifying "arm" to something more "BR-like" (candidate: "part").

## Two findings

1. **Positive collaboration effect on the human (WR data).** Pairing with the agent is **upskilling BR's
   English** as a side-effect: the agent's vocabulary transfers to BR over time. A genuine, measurable effect
   of the human-AI collaboration on the *human* half of the pair, worth naming alongside the usual
   agent-side effects. (BR named it as WR data himself.)

2. **Register-mismatch regression (actionable, the lesson).** The agent imported **research jargon**
   ("arm", and by extension "ablation", "negative control", "decoy") into a **BR-voice publication** (the
   blog), whose brief explicitly wants **no esoteric academic lingo** and BR's plain, accessible register
   (developer-first, laymen can digest some). Research jargon belongs in the **formal writeup**
   (`047-writeup.md`), anchored where used; it should **not leak into BR's voice** in the blog. This is a
   sibling of the publication-register disciplines already pinned ([[br-dislikes-em-dashes]],
   [[br-bad-speller-fix-in-publications]], [[educate-lead-with-one-screen]]): same class of fix (keep the
   publication sounding like BR), new dimension (vocabulary register, not just em-dash / spelling / layout).

## Action

- **Blog:** replace "arm" with a BR-simple word ("part", or "half"/"side" where the two-story framing is
  explicit); do a jargon sweep for other leaked research terms. Keep "arm" etc. in the formal writeup.
- **Standing:** in BR-voice publications, prefer BR's plain register; the agent's academic vocabulary is fine
  in formal research docs (anchored) and can *teach* BR in chat, but must not leak into his published voice.
  Pinned as memory [[publications-match-br-register]].
