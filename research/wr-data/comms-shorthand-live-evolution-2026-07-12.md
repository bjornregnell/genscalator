# WR specimen: comms-shorthand evolving live during a racing burst (2026-07-12)

Two comms-protocol observations BR flagged during a fast, overlapping-feed working burst.

## 1. The `|sv` cue grew a translation-help "dance-twist"

The `|sv` suffix marks a Swedish word ([[cue-sv-swedish-marker]]). During this session BR clarified a NEW sense:
in OFFICIAL docs (genscalator skills, blogs, PRD), a `|sv`-tagged word often means "a great Swedish word BR knows
but isn't sure of the good English for" - so the agent should render it in **good English** (not preserve the
Swedish), and **chat about options** if the rendering isn't straightforward. BR verbatim: "the |sv pipe-to-swedish
cue actually means that in official docs ... agent should help to translate that into good english and if it is not
straightforward agent should chat about options."

Live examples this session: `efterapa|sv`, `kakofoni|sv`, `buller|sv` (rendered "noise" in the PRD). BR: "we have
a new dance-twist."

**Why it matters (SM054, RQ0/RQ1/RQ2).** A clean division-of-labour asymmetry: the HUMAN holds the concept (the
Swedish word he knows precisely) but not always the English lexical retrieval; the AGENT holds the English
rendering. Neither alone produces the good official-doc phrasing; together they do - an RQ2 escalation beat inside
the comms channel itself, and concrete RQ0 evidence (a real, recurring asymmetry in human-agent conversation).
Pinned to foundations (the `|sv` cue entry) + memory.

## 2. "We are racing" - the overlapping-feed dynamic, observed

BR fired a rapid burst of small edits and asks faster than the agent could process one before the next arrived,
then explicitly cued "we are racing; i'll rest to avoid confusion" and went quiet to let the agent eat the queue
([[cue-we-are-racing]]).

**Why it matters.** A concrete instance of a human-agent turn-taking / bandwidth asymmetry: the human can emit
faster than the (serial, per-turn) agent consumes, so the channel congests; the human's mitigation is to REST
(stop emitting) so the agent catches up and reconciles. The agent's counterpart duty: keep useful work going,
treat a rapid near-identical pair as one edited message ([[harness-double-post-edit-race]]), and reconcile the
WHOLE queue rather than answering each fragment. Another RQ0/RQ1 datapoint (a turn-taking asymmetry), with "rest to
avoid confusion" as the human-side mitigation.
