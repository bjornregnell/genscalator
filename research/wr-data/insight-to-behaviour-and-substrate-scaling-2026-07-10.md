## 1. "A changed understanding is new data" -> how does understanding become BEHAVIOUR?
BR flags the phrase (from [[raw-data-append-only]], used when appending the use-vs-rot reframe) as a good
introspective observation that opens a **deeper question: how is a new understanding best manifested into good
behaviour?** - the conversion of insight -> *enacted* change, across three loci:
- **the agent** - a logged insight is knowledge; enacting it is behaviour (the recalled != enacted gap we hit
  live this session);
- **the substrate** - how an insight gets encoded so it RELIABLY changes behaviour (structural > knowledge
  safeguard: a hook / tool / allowlist beats a memory line);
- **the joint agent+human "co-operative behaviour"** - how the PAIR learns a *new cooperative pattern*, not
  just each side alone (e.g. the go-dial, the dances - protocols the pair enacts together).

This is the post-training-learning problem (blog 007 "learning beyond the inference barrier"): model weights
are fixed, so ALL durable learning lives in the substrate + habits + the pair's protocols. Open question:
what makes an insight STICK as behaviour vs evaporate as an inert note? (structural encoding, prosthetic
habits, foundations rules, dances).

(instruction-adherence-decay = the enactment-decay side), RT048 (substrate-content power over behaviour), and

## 2. The shared substrate has outgrown the human - a role reversal, and the efficiency wall
BR observes: **foundations.md has grown so rich that BR (the human) now needs the AGENT's help to stay
consistent with it.** A **role reversal** - usually the agent needs help holding context; now the shared
foundations exceed the *human's* working memory, so the agent must help the human remain consistent with their
own accumulated doctrine. This points at an **efficiency wall**: reaching the next level of the joint project
needs higher joint efficiency - the pair must get better at *leveraging* the (now large) substrate, or it
turns from multiplier into drag.

BR's forward vision (underspecified one-liner, for AFTER a first working super-harness pilot for Claude):
**"generalize the super-harness to open-source harnesses (such as open code) and get untangled from the secret
sauce of Anthropic."** I.e. **harness-level digital sovereignty** - the super-harness approach
(capability-clamp + tap/inject + dashboard) should not be locked to Anthropic's proprietary runtime; generalize
it to open-source agent harnesses so the method survives independent of one vendor. The harness-level analogue
of the SM035 code-hosting sovereignty / EU-sovereignty ethos.
