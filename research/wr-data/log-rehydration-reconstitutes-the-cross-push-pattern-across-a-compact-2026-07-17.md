# Log-rehydration reconstitutes a minion's cross-push PATTERN across a compact (2026-07-17)

**The cycle-3 result, and the strongest thing the meta-minion study has produced about longevity.**

## What happened

Meta-minion push 3 ran on a **freshly spawned** fable `general-purpose` sub-agent, **re-hydrated ONLY from the
written log** — told to Read `meta-minion-brief.md` + `minion-log/push-1.md` + `minion-log/push-2.md` — with
**no live context and no transcript resume** (a second `/compact` had passed since push 2). It then extended
the study's cross-push **PATTERN** to a **third instance**:

> *"Scope over-extension of a TRUE property — third specimen … Push 1: `add`-only validation asserted
> tool-wide. Push 2: MED-only quote-awareness asserted flat. Push 3: 'reuses landing copy' true for
> tagline+about, widened to `name`. Same signature, collapsing severity."*

It cited pushes 1 and 2 **by their specifics** to build specimen 3.

## Why it matters — the payoff of "long-lived" is a property of the SUBSTRATE, not of a living context

The milestone doc (`does-long-lived-pay-off`) named the **PATTERN slot** as the one thing a fresh minion
**cannot** produce: *"'twice, same signature' is a FINDING, and it requires a memory that spans both … a fresh
minion spawned for push 2 would have had nothing to compare it to."* **This cycle qualifies that claim
sharply:** a fresh minion **re-hydrated from the log** produced the cross-push pattern. So the payoff is a
property of the **durable, curated substrate** (`push-1.md`, `push-2.md`), not of a continuously-living
context.

This extends the compact-survival finding one step:
- Compact-survival test found *"long-lived" is really a durable **transcript** + a re-hydrator*.
- **This cycle:** even the transcript is not needed — the **curated log** suffices, and it is far smaller and
  cheaper than a transcript, and it is the thing the write-to-own-dir design already produces.
- ⇒ **The day's load-bearing finding, confirmed on the instrument itself:** only externalized-AND-reloaded
  substrate survives a warp — and here it was the minion's *memory of its own findings* that survived, via the
  log.

## Strategic consequence — the study stops depending on fable `SendMessage` resume

SM085(a)/SM081 measured fable `SendMessage` resume at **0/2**, a standing threat to a "long-lived" fable
minion (`long-lived-meta-minion.md` §10, 2026-07-17 00:4x). **Fresh-spawn-from-log sidesteps it entirely:**
we no longer need transcript-resume to work for fable, because the log carries the cross-push memory. The
"long-lived" design is **reconstituted, not continuous** — and reconstitution is more robust (inspectable,
cheap, survives arbitrary warps). Validates T2 mitigations **1** (`minion-log/` IS the resume prompt) and **3**
(restart cost measured: the pattern-carry cost was ~0 for a well-recorded pattern).

## Honest bounds

- **n=1 pattern-carry.** One pattern, extended once.
- **The pattern was WELL-recorded** in push-2 (explicit signature + countermeasure). This tests *"can a
  re-hydrated minion EXTEND a cleanly-recorded pattern"* (yes), **not** *"can it rediscover a faint or
  implicit one"* (untested — plausibly the harder, log-quality-sensitive case). ⭐ Consistent with T2
  mitigation 2: **log QUALITY is the dial** — a well-written prior carried at ~0 cost; a poorly-written one
  would not.
- Does not overturn that a *continuously-live* context could carry MORE (nuance the log omits). It shows the
  log carries **enough for the headline artifact**.
