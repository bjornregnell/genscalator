# WR specimen: mutual non-offense + the anthropomorphizing typing-tax (2026-07-12)

**Context.** During a register-feedback exchange (BR had rewritten - "slaughtered" - a bloated agent-drafted
PR review comment), BR added a playful aside and tagged it "WR data".

**BR, verbatim-ish:** "we both know per asymmetry that you cant offend me and i cant offend you; (well a human
could get offended but that's anthropomorphizing (aaargh that hurts to type can we find a 4 letter word for that
(dream on human...))) ;)"

## Two beats worth logging

1. **Mutual non-offense as an affective-asymmetry corollary.** BR's model: the agent cannot be offended (no
   feelings to hurt), and BR treats the agent's drafts as non-personal, so he is not offended either - he
   "slaughters" freely. The asymmetry: a human CAN be offended; attributing offense-capacity to the agent is the
   anthropomorphizing error. Consequence: candid mutual critique is cheap - the agent's lack of ego is a genuine
   collaboration *affordance* of the asymmetry (blunt, fast review with no face-saving overhead). Feeds SM054
   category A (differences: affect) and arguably category C (a positive effect: asymmetry-awareness enabling a
   faster, franker feedback loop). Sibling: [[laughter-and-the-affective-channel-asymmetry]].

2. **The anthropomorphizing typing-tax (a language/UX micro-friction).** BR: "anthropomorphizing" "hurts to
   type", a half-joking request for a 4-letter word, self-answered "(dream on human...)". A real ergonomic tax:
   a high-frequency concept in this work has no short handle, so every use costs keystrokes and breaks flow.
   Same family as blog 004's "language tax" (having to avoid the p-word) and the de-trigger-words idea. Short
   handles the agent floated: the verb "to ape" (3 letters - to ape human traits onto the non-human; not
   literally correct but short, with an almost-human resonance), or a typed cue like `|anth`. OPEN: adopt a
   shorthand for the recurring concept?
