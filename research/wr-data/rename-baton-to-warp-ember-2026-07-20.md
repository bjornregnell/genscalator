# Rename: baton → warp ember (2026-07-20)

**Decision (BR, 2026-07-20 ~14:30):** the warp handover artifact formerly called *baton* is renamed
**warp ember** (short form *ember*; files `EMBER-*.md`). The term had been BR's settle on 2026-07-17
over the agent's coinage "carrier"; both renames happened while the artifact's SM168 experiments were
still unrun — coined terms stay mutable until first deploy.

## Why

1. **The Swedish problem (BR's trigger):** *batong* in Swedish is the police truncheon — "en sådan som
   polisen slår olydiga i huvudet med". For a bilingual pair whose chat is often Swedish, the word
   carried violence into every handover sentence. English "baton" is itself split between conductor's
   stick, relay baton, and truncheon.
2. **The smithy fit (why *ember* won the brainstorm):** a smith *banks the fire* at closing — ash over
   the coals so an ember survives the night, blown back into flame in the morning. That is the warp,
   move for move: exit banks the session; the fresh agent blows the ember back into working context.
   The metaphor also keeps the load-bearing discontinuity point the relay metaphor had (different
   runners → the fire dies, only the ember crosses) AND lands inside genscalator's existing forge
   vocabulary (`tt forge`). Bonus un-planned coherence: weaving's *warp* (sv. *varp*) is the
   continuous threads — the substrate — through which each session is woven as weft; the ember is
   carried along the warp threads to the next pick.
3. **Candidates considered** (in-feed brainstorm, families): fire (ember, kindling, pilot light),
   culture (sourdough starter), orders (brief, charter), travel (relay, booster, capsule, primer).
   BR picked ember on the blacksmith fit; "warp brief" was the runner-up for its built-in
   one-screen/read-first semantics — the template keeps "brief-like" §0 behaviour regardless of name.

## Terminology rules

- Full term in prose and titles: **warp ember** (first mention explains it); short form *ember*.
- Files: `EMBER-TEMPLATE.md`, `EMBER-EXAMPLE.md` (short names; the H1 carries "warp ember").
- **Raw stays raw:** wr-data specimens, minion-log pushes, RAW-DATA, and the verbatim specimen inside
  EMBER-EXAMPLE keep the word "baton" as written — retract/rename by annotating, never by rewriting
  history. This note is the annotation they point to.
