# Topical drift while tired: the tangential wander as a candidate fatigue gauge (2026-07-10)

BR-flagged WR datum + hypothesis. Late in a long session, tired (`:Z`), BR's conversation DRIFTED into a run of
topically-adjacent-but-off-task tangents (the Anglo name-drop tic, "what is mirth", the laughter meta, Swedish
loanwords). BR named it himself mid-stream ("never mind I am tired and my mind goes into tangents"), then proposed

## The idea
**Topical drift** - wandering off the task / genscalator spine into meta / associative tangents - as a
**human-psyche behavioral proxy for fatigue**, alongside the ones already noted: typo rate (the
typo-as-fatigue-gauge), the `:Z` self-report, cue-mix, session duration. Feeds the live-dashboard thread

## Echt caveats (this is a HYPOTHESIS, per the human-psyche honesty rule)
- **BR's own framing: "maybe, don't know, just an idea."** Not validated; human-psyche mechanisms here are
  hypotheses, not tested (`wr-data/README` honesty rule).
- **Drift is a NOISY, double-edged signal, not purely fatigue.** Topical drift is also relaxed, playful,
  associative, exploratory thinking - and here the tangents were genuinely GENERATIVE: the "off-task" wander
  produced real research (the laughter / affective-asymmetry note, the Anglo-register learning, this note).
  Breaks and tangents *incubate* ([[cue-brb]]: "breaks may spawn new ideas"). So drift correlates with fatigue
  but also with valuable loosened thinking; **best used as a COMPOSITE** (drift + `:Z` + rising typo rate +
  late-session clock), never alone.
- **Member-check anchor:** the strongest version pairs the observable drift with the human's own report - BR
  saying "I'm tired, my mind goes into tangents" IS the ground-truth confirmation (RT001: the human is ground
  truth for his own state). Drift-without-self-report is a hypothesis-flag, never a diagnosis.
